Panda

This concept revolves around the idea that technology can only be a viable and beneficial solution to the planet by making it as eco-friendly as possible.

"Panda" is a project the carries this idea and evolves the known concept of personal computing into a platform so versatile it can easily manage everyday tasks or even high performance ones flawlessly â€“ by using the best and latest in organic computer components and technology, smart assembly processes such as SMT, and delivering it as a completely modular solution.

The point in making "Panda" eco-friendly revolves around the actual design: making it form a maximum of recycled / reusable / reused and high quality materials, involving new content only when needed within the production.

Its complete build (components, shell and sub-assemblies) is hardware-free, thus making it a painless and tool-less platform to manage for even the most average of consumers.

Industrial design, for me, is the best way to express my creativity while actually putting it to good use in developing system and product concepts that hopefully will change the world we live in for the better.

The environment is a particular concern of mine, as it binds another interest that I cherish: technology.

"Panda" is one of the concepts which interprets the industry's full potential for better and greener technology, bundled in the same user-friendly package we are slowly getting familiar with: mobile computing.

Basically, the idea in my head revolves around the fact that the way our capitalist system introduces technology is simply done in a manner that suits benefits to the corporations, completely disregarding planetary resource management, and designing for the trash and stockpile.

Taking the personal computer as my main critic and in respect to the tremendous evolution its field has undergone with multi-core processing, super-threading, miniaturization of the platform and its construction processes, how in hell are we still stuck with a "crapload" of soldered components, that scream for the trash can when something goes wrong?

Why hasn't the green SMT alternative taken over yet? How is it that we are not printing our electronics on recyclable substrates rather than abiding by the chemically driven processes that are required for PCBs to take shape?

Those are only some of the many problems I wish to tackle during my career and lifetime, and to which the "Panda" initiative hopefully started to answer.
